Below are the most common questions about rTMS/iTBS in clinical practice, and what patients usually want to know before their first session at Smart CNS Center.

---

### Does rTMS hurt?
Treatment is not painful. During magnetic pulse delivery, you feel a light "tapping" sensation at the scalp contact point and hear the sound of the magnet (similar to an MRI scanner). During the first 2–3 sessions, there may be mild skin discomfort, which usually disappears as the body becomes accustomed to the sensation. Rarely, a mild headache may occur and resolves quickly with common analgesics.

### Is rTMS safe?
Yes. rTMS is fully approved by the two major medicine and device regulators (FDA in the United States and EMA in Europe). It is a very safe method used internationally for more than 15 years. The main but extremely rare side effect is seizure risk (approximately 1 in 30,000 sessions), which is why detailed history is taken before starting. It does not produce the systemic side effects of antidepressants.

### Does it require anesthesia or hospitalization?
No. The patient sits in a comfortable chair and remains 100% awake throughout treatment. No anesthesia, sedation, or preparation is required (not even fasting). As soon as the session ends, you can leave the clinic, drive, and continue your normal day without any effect on mental clarity.

### How long does each session last?
Duration depends entirely on the selected treatment protocol. With the most modern protocols (such as iTBS), the active treatment lasts **only 3 to 5 minutes**. With classic high-frequency rTMS protocols, the session may last from 19 to almost 40 minutes.

### When will I begin to see results?
Although every person responds differently, clinical experience shows that most patients begin to notice symptom improvement (such as increased energy, better mood, and reduced anxiety) after the **10th to 15th session**. It is normal not to see dramatic changes during the first week.

### Can I continue my medication or work?
Yes. rTMS can be safely combined with the medication you are already taking — in fact, in most cases it starts as an "adjunctive" treatment alongside existing treatment. Also, no time off work is required. You can have a session during a break and return immediately.
