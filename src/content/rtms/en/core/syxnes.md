The most frequent questions about rTMS/iTBS in clinical practice. Read below what our patients usually want to know before their first session at Smart CNS Center.

### Does rTMS hurt?

The treatment is not painful. During delivery of the magnetic pulses, you will feel a light tapping sensation at the contact point on the scalp and hear the sound of the magnet, similar to an MRI scanner. During the first 2–3 sessions, there may be mild skin discomfort, which usually disappears as the body becomes accustomed to the sensation. In rare cases, a mild headache may occur and usually resolves promptly with common analgesics.

### Is rTMS safe?

Yes. rTMS is fully approved by the two largest medicine and device regulatory bodies, the FDA in the United States and the EMA in Europe. It is a very safe method that has been used internationally for more than fifteen years. The main but extremely rare side effect is the risk of seizure, approximately 1 in 30,000 sessions, which is why a detailed medical history is taken before treatment begins. It does not produce the systemic side effects associated with antidepressants.

### Does it require anaesthesia or hospital admission?

No, not at all. The patient sits in a comfortable chair and remains fully awake throughout the treatment. No form of anaesthesia, sedation, or preparation is required, including fasting. Once the session is complete, you can leave the clinic immediately, drive, and continue your normal day without any effect on mental clarity.

### How long does each session last?

The duration depends entirely on the selected treatment protocol. With newer protocols, such as iTBS, the main treatment lasts only 3 to 5 minutes. With classical high-frequency rTMS protocols, the session may last from 19 to almost 40 minutes.

### When will I start seeing results?

Although every person responds differently, clinical experience shows that most patients begin to notice improvement in their symptoms — such as increased energy, improved mood, and reduced anxiety — after the 10th to 15th session. It is entirely normal not to see major changes during the first week.

### Can I continue my medication or my work?

Yes. rTMS can be combined safely with the medication you are already taking; in fact, in most cases it begins as an adjunctive treatment added to the current treatment plan. It also does not require taking leave from work. You can have the session during your break and return immediately.
