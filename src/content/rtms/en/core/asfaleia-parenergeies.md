Repetitive transcranial magnetic stimulation (rTMS) is an approved, non-invasive treatment with a well-documented safety profile. [Learn exactly what rTMS and iTBS are](/en/rtms/core/ti-einai). It has been used clinically for more than 25 years, and the available data — from both clinical studies and international clinical practice — confirm that it is one of the safest options within the spectrum of mental health treatments.

This page clearly answers the questions patients commonly ask before starting treatment: what they may feel, what is rare, and in which cases the treatment is not indicated.

---

## Common side effects

The side effects of rTMS are generally mild, local, and temporary. They mainly appear at the beginning of treatment and subside as the body becomes accustomed to the stimulation.

### Headache or pressure sensation in the skull

This is the most common side effect, reported by approximately 1 in 3 patients during the first sessions. It is usually mild, located around the stimulation area, and resolves within a few hours — usually without analgesics. In most cases, it decreases or disappears after the first 3–5 sessions.

### Local scalp discomfort

During the session, some patients feel a light tapping or tingling sensation at the contact point. It is not painful and stops immediately when the session ends.

### Facial muscle contractions

Some patients notice mild involuntary contractions of muscles near the stimulation site during the session. This is expected, is not a sign of a problem, and resolves automatically.

### Mild fatigue

Some patients report tiredness after the first sessions. It does not require rest or avoidance of daily activities.

---

## Rare side effects

### Seizure

This is the most serious possible complication, with an estimated frequency below 0.1% — fewer than 1 in 1,000 treatment courses — in appropriately selected patients. The risk is minimized through proper pre-assessment and strict exclusion of patients with a history of epilepsy or other risk factors. At Smart CNS Center, every patient is thoroughly assessed before treatment begins.

### Mood changes during treatment

Rarely, some patients with bipolar disorder may experience a change in emotional state during a treatment course. For this reason, monitoring during treatment is an integral part of the protocol.

### Hearing

The rTMS mechanism produces a repeated clicking sound. Although the intensity is not considered harmful, using earplugs during the session is standard precautionary practice.

## Contraindications — Who cannot undergo rTMS

Assessment of contraindications is a mandatory step before every treatment.

**Absolute contraindications:**

- Metallic implants inside or near the skull, such as vascular clips, cochlear implants, or neurostimulators.
- Older-generation cardiac pacemaker or implantable defibrillator.

**Relative contraindications, assessed case by case:**

- Epilepsy.
- Severe history of head injury.
- Concurrent use of medications that lower the seizure threshold.

In every case, the final decision about suitability is made after a complete clinical assessment. [See whether you may be an appropriate candidate for rTMS](/en/rtms/core/kostos-kai-diadikasia).

## rTMS vs pharmacotherapy: comparison of safety profile

| Feature | rTMS | Antidepressants |
|---|---|---|
| Systemic side effects | None | Common, including weight change, sexual dysfunction, gastrointestinal symptoms |
| Drug interactions | None | Possible |
| Dependence / withdrawal | None | Possible with some medications |
| Requires anaesthesia | No | — |
| Requires hospital admission | No | — |
| Time to clinical effect | 2–4 weeks | 2–6 weeks |

## Safety in special populations

### Older patients

rTMS can be applied safely in older adults. Stimulation intensity is adjusted according to each patient’s motor threshold, ensuring an individualized and safe dose.

### Patients taking multiple medications

Because rTMS does not interact pharmacologically with other substances, it can be applied alongside existing medication without changing it — unless clinically indicated otherwise.

## Safety procedure at Smart CNS Center

Before each treatment course, we follow these steps:

1. Complete clinical assessment and medical history.
2. Completion of a standardized rTMS safety questionnaire.
3. Measurement of resting motor threshold (rMT) to individualize stimulation intensity.
4. Patient information and written consent.

During treatment, the patient remains fully awake, does not need to fast, and can drive and return to daily activities immediately after the session. [See in detail how rTMS is performed in practice](/en/rtms/core/ti-einai).

## Frequently asked questions about rTMS safety

**Is rTMS painful?** No. Most patients describe a tapping sensation on the scalp during the session. If the sensation is uncomfortable, the intensity can be adjusted.

**Can I drive after the session?** Yes. rTMS does not affect reflexes, alertness, or driving ability.

**Is there a risk of permanent brain damage?** There is no evidence suggesting structural damage to brain tissue from clinical application of rTMS using approved protocols and intensities.

**How safe is it compared with medication?** The safety profile of rTMS — absence of systemic side effects, interactions, and dependence — makes it a particularly attractive option for patients who do not tolerate antidepressants or are looking for a medication-free treatment alternative.
