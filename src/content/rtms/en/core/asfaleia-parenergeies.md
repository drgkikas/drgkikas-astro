## Introduction

Repetitive Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (rTMS) is an approved, non-invasive treatment with a well-documented safety profile. Learn [what exactly rTMS and iTBS are](/en/rtms/core/ti-einai). It has been used clinically for more than 25 years, and available data — from both clinical studies and international clinical practice — confirm that it is one of the safest options within the spectrum of mental health treatments.

This page clearly answers the questions patients often ask before starting treatment: what they may feel, what is rare, and in which cases treatment is not indicated.

---

## Common side effects

rTMS side effects are usually mild, local, and transient. They occur mainly at the beginning of treatment and subside as the body becomes accustomed to the stimulation.

### Headache or scalp tension
This is the most common side effect, reported by approximately 1 in 3 patients during the first sessions. It is usually mild, localized to the stimulation area, and resolves within a few hours — usually without analgesics. In most cases, it decreases or disappears after the first 3–5 sessions.

### Local scalp discomfort
During the session, some patients feel a light tapping or tingling sensation at the contact point. It is not painful and stops immediately when the session ends.

### Facial muscle twitches
Some patients notice mild involuntary twitches in muscles near the stimulation site during the session. This is expected, does not indicate a problem, and resolves automatically.

### Mild fatigue
Some patients report tiredness after the first sessions. This does not require rest or avoidance of daily activities.

---

## Rare side effects

### Seizure
This is the most serious possible complication, with an estimated frequency below 0.1% (fewer than 1 in 1,000 treatment courses) in appropriately selected patients. The risk is minimized through proper pre-assessment and strict exclusion of patients with epilepsy history or other risk factors. At Smart CNS Center, every patient is thoroughly assessed before treatment begins.

### Mood changes during treatment
Rarely, some patients with bipolar disorder may experience a change in mood state during a treatment course. For this reason, monitoring during treatment is an integral part of the protocol.

### Hearing
The rTMS device produces a repeated clicking sound. Although its intensity is not considered harmful, use of earplugs during the session is standard precautionary practice.

## Contraindications — who cannot undergo rTMS

Assessment of contraindications is mandatory before any treatment.

**Absolute contraindications:**
- Metallic implants inside or near the skull (e.g., vascular clips, cochlear implants, neurostimulators)
- Older-generation cardiac pacemaker or implantable defibrillator

**Relative contraindications (assessed case by case):**
- Epilepsy
- Severe history of head injury
- Concurrent use of medication that lowers the seizure threshold

In all cases, the final decision on suitability is made after full clinical assessment. See [whether you are a suitable candidate for rTMS](/en/rtms/).

## rTMS vs pharmacotherapy: safety profile comparison

| Feature | rTMS | Antidepressants |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Systemic side effects** | None | Common (weight, sexual dysfunction, gastrointestinal) |
| **Drug interactions** | None | Possible |
| **Dependence / withdrawal** | None | Possible with some medications |
| **Requires anesthesia** | No | — |
| **Requires hospitalization** | No | — |
| **Time to effect** | 2–4 weeks | 2–6 weeks |

## Safety in special populations

### Older patients
rTMS can be applied safely in older patients. Stimulation intensity is adjusted according to each patient’s motor threshold, ensuring individualized and safe dosing.

### Patients receiving multiple medications
Because rTMS has no pharmacological interaction with other substances, it can be applied alongside existing medication without changing it, unless clinically indicated otherwise.

## Safety procedure at Smart CNS Center

Before each treatment course, we follow these steps:
1. Full clinical assessment and medical history
2. Completion of a standardized rTMS safety questionnaire
3. Measurement of resting motor threshold (rMT) for intensity personalization
4. Patient information and written consent

During treatment, the patient remains fully awake, does not need to fast, and can drive and return to daily activities immediately after the session. See [how rTMS treatment works in practice](/en/rtms/core/kostos-kai-diadikasia).

## Safety FAQ

**Is rTMS painful?**
No. Most patients describe a tapping sensation on the scalp during the session. If the sensation is uncomfortable, intensity can be adjusted.

**Can I drive after the session?**
Yes. rTMS does not affect reflexes, alertness, or driving ability.

**Is there a risk of permanent brain damage?**
There is no evidence suggesting structural brain tissue damage from clinical rTMS use when approved protocols and intensities are applied.

**How safe is it compared with medication?**
The rTMS safety profile — with no systemic side effects, drug interactions, or dependence — makes it an especially attractive option for patients who cannot tolerate antidepressants or are seeking a [medication-free alternative](/en/rtms/).
