The decision to start neuromodulation treatment, whether rTMS or iTBS, is an investment in your health. The purpose of this page is to provide, from the outset, a complete and transparent overview of the practical steps, the time required, and the way cost is determined, without unnecessary medical jargon or vague “packages.”

---

## The process at a glance — logistics

- **What the initial assessment includes:** A complete psychiatric and medical interview, lasting approximately 60 minutes. We record your history, the medication treatments you have tried, and assess whether rTMS is truly the appropriate and safe option for you. We never start sessions without medical certainty.
- **How many sessions are usually needed:** Depending on the condition, a protocol may last from 10 to 30 sessions. Usually, 20–30 sessions are required for treatment-resistant depression and 29 for OCD.
- **How long each session lasts:** Depending on the technology applied, each session may last from 3 minutes with newer iTBS protocols to 40 minutes with classical 10 Hz rTMS. Once the session is complete, you return immediately to your activities — driving and working normally, without any anaesthesia.
- **When cost and the plan are discussed:** The exact treatment plan and financial cost are explained after the initial assessment, when we know precisely what your brain needs.

---

## What affects the final plan and cost

In our approach, there are no ready-made packages. The final cost is determined exclusively by three clinical factors:

1. **The exact diagnosis:** Depression, obsessive-compulsive disorder, chronic pain, and addictions require different brain target areas and different stimulation intensity or session duration.
2. **The selected protocol:** We use latest-generation equipment that allows protocol selection. Three-minute iTBS or deep TMS will determine a different time structure for the patient.
3. **The patient’s response:** Many patients show marked remission by the 15th session and stop. Others may need extension up to 30 sessions, or 1–2 maintenance sessions per month to help prevent relapse.

**Note:** rTMS is currently not covered by EOPYY. However, some private insurance contracts may provide partial coverage. We recommend checking your policy.

---

## What to expect at the first visit

The initial medical assessment is the most important step before starting rTMS. It is important to come prepared. Below is exactly what you will be asked to provide:

- Bring a detailed list of the medications you are currently taking, including exact dosages.
- Be able to briefly report which pharmacological treatments you have tried in the past and whether they caused side effects that forced you to stop them.
- Inform us immediately if you have any non-removable metal objects in the head or neck, such as vascular clips or pumps, a pacemaker, or a medical history of epileptic seizures.
- Any recent MRI scans or electroencephalograms (EEGs) should be provided to the physician.

After taking your history, we spend time measuring your motor threshold (**Motor Threshold / rMT**). This is the absolutely necessary, painless test that calibrates the intensity of the magnetic pulse to your own brain, ensuring maximum safety and effectiveness.
