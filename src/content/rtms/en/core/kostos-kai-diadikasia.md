The decision to start neuromodulation treatment (rTMS/iTBS) is an investment in your health. This page aims to give you a complete and transparent view from the start regarding practical steps, time, and how cost is determined, without medical jargon or vague "packages."

---

## The procedure at a glance (Logistics)

- **What the initial assessment includes:** A full psychiatric and medical interview (approximately 60 minutes). We record your history, the medications you have tried, and assess whether rTMS is truly an appropriate and safe option for you. We never start sessions without medical certainty.
- **How many sessions are usually needed:** Depending on the condition, a protocol may include **10 to 30 sessions**. (Usually 20–30 sessions for Treatment-Resistant Depression, 29 for OCD).
- **How long each session lasts:** Depending on the technology applied, the session may last from **3 minutes** (newer iTBS protocols) up to **40 minutes** (classic 10 Hz rTMS). When it ends, you return immediately to your activities — including driving and working normally, without anesthesia.
- **When cost and the plan are discussed:** The exact treatment plan and financial cost are analyzed **after the initial assessment**, once we know exactly what your brain needs.

---

## What affects the final plan (and cost)

In our approach, there are no "ready-made packages." The final cost is determined exclusively by three clinical factors:

1. **The precise diagnosis:** Depression, Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder, chronic pain, and addictions require different brain target areas and different session intensity or duration.
2. **The selected protocol:** Latest-generation equipment allows protocol choice. Three-minute iTBS (Theta Burst) or Deep TMS imply different time structures for the patient.
3. **Patient response:** Many patients show excellent remission by the 15th session and treatment can stop. Others may need extension to 30 sessions, or 1–2 monthly maintenance sessions to prevent relapse.

*Note: rTMS is currently not covered by EOPYY. Some private insurance policies may offer partial coverage; we recommend checking your policy.*

---

## What to expect at the first visit

The initial medical assessment is the most important step before starting rTMS. It is important to come prepared. Learn below what exactly will be required:

- Bring a **detailed list of all current medications** (and exact doses).
- Be able to briefly describe which pharmacotherapies you **tried in the past** and whether side effects forced discontinuation.
- Inform us immediately if you have **non-removable metallic objects** in the head or neck (e.g., vascular clips, pumps), a pacemaker, or a history of epileptic seizures.
- Provide any recent MRI scans or EEG reports to the physician.

After taking the history, we spend time measuring your **Motor Threshold (rMT)**. This is an essential, painless test that "calibrates" magnetic pulse intensity to your own brain, ensuring maximum safety and effectiveness.
