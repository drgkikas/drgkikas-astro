Repetitive Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (rTMS) is an FDA-approved, non-invasive medical treatment that uses strong magnetic fields to selectively stimulate nerve cells in the brain. **iTBS (Intermittent Theta Burst Stimulation)** is its technological evolution: a protocol that can deliver similarly robust therapeutic effects in only 3 minutes.

Both methods are performed in the clinic, while the patient remains fully awake, **without any need for anesthesia, systemic pharmacotherapy, or hospitalization**, providing a highly safe alternative for treatment-resistant depression and obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD).

---

## Brief definition: rTMS

rTMS is based on principles of physics and electromagnetism. A special coil is placed on the patient’s scalp. This coil produces a brief and strong changing magnetic field (similar to that of an MRI scanner, approximately 1.5–2.5 Tesla).

The magnetic field passes through the skull unobstructed and generates a very small electrical current in neurons of the underlying cortex. By repeating these pulses, the psychiatrist can directly influence specific brain regions, increasing or decreasing excitability (such as in the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex). This activates **neuroplasticity** and helps restore normal function of neural networks involved in depression.

---

## What is the difference between rTMS and iTBS?

The main difference lies in **treatment time** and **pulse pattern**:

- **Classic rTMS (10 Hz):** delivers magnetic pulses at a constant frequency of 10 pulses per second. A classic depression session lasts from 19 to almost **40 minutes**.
- **iTBS (theta burst):** instead of constant pulses, it delivers "bursts" of pulses at a specific rhythm (theta rhythm, 5 Hz), which resembles a natural communication frequency of the brain (hippocampal theta rhythm). Because of this biological "compatibility", iTBS can achieve the same enhancement of neuroplasticity in **only 3 minutes**.

Both protocols have similar efficacy (as shown in the large THREE-D clinical trial in 2018), but iTBS is substantially more patient-friendly because it requires minimal time in the treatment chair.

---

## What happens during a session?

The procedure is designed to cause minimal disruption to daily life:

1. **No preparation is required:** you may eat and drink normally before coming to the center.
2. **Comfort during treatment:** you sit in a special comfortable chair (similar to a dental chair) while fully awake.
3. **Application:** the physician accurately identifies the target area on your head. During stimulation, you hear an intermittent sound (similar to the clicking sound of an MRI scanner) and feel a light "tapping" sensation on the scalp. It is not painful, although there may be brief discomfort during the first sessions.
4. **Immediate return:** once the 3-minute iTBS protocol (or the corresponding protocol) is completed, you stand up and can immediately drive or return to work. There is no effect on mental clarity.

---

## Who is a candidate for treatment?

Treatment (either classic rTMS or iTBS) is mainly indicated for:

- Patients with **Treatment-Resistant Depression (TRD)**, meaning they have already tried at least two different antidepressants without satisfactory benefit.
- Patients who cannot tolerate **medication side effects** (such as gastrointestinal symptoms, weight gain, or sexual dysfunction).
- Patients with **Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)** who show resistance to pharmacotherapy.
- Situations where medication use may involve risks, such as **pregnancy**.

> **Who cannot undergo rTMS:** people with non-removable metallic objects in the head or neck (e.g., cochlear implants, aneurysm clips), or with an active medical history of epilepsy or seizures.
