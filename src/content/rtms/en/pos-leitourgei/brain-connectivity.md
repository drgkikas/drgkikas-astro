Brain connectivity refers to the ways in which brain regions communicate — either through white matter anatomical pathways (structural connectivity) or through coordinated activity in the absence of external stimulus (functional connectivity). rTMS does not only affect the site of stimulation: it reshapes entire networks through [network plasticity](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/neuroplasticity).

### Why it matters
Depression, anxiety, and many psychiatric disorders are now understood as connectivity disorders — not as a lesion in a single isolated structure. This means that treatment must target the network, not just the node. [Circuit neuromodulation](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/neuromodulation) via rTMS, applied to the DLPFC, transmits stimulation to the entire cortico-limbic network.

### Mechanism
- **Structural connectivity:** Bundles of myelinated axons (white matter tracts) — measured with DTI (Diffusion Tensor Imaging).
- **Functional connectivity (FC):** Correlations in the BOLD signal (fMRI) between distant areas at rest (resting-state fMRI).
- **Default Mode Network (DMN):** Active at rest — hyperconnectivity in depression = rumination.
- **Central Executive Network (CEN):** DLPFC + posterior parietal cortex — hypoconnectivity in MDD.
- **Salience Network:** ACC + insula — dysregulated in many psychiatric disorders.
- rTMS on the L-DLPFC increases functional connectivity (FC) between DLPFC and sgACC (areas with negative correlation) — this change correlates with clinical improvement.

### Clinical Significance
**Predictive biomarker:** Initial negative correlation between DLPFC-sgACC predicts antidepressant response to rTMS (Fox et al., 2012; Drysdale et al., 2017). See more [frequently asked questions about connectivity](/en/rtms/core/syxnes).

**Subgroup identification:** Connectivity-based biotypes identified via fMRI identify which patients with [treatment-resistant depression (TRD)](/en/rtms/endixeis/trd) will benefit most from which rTMS protocol.
