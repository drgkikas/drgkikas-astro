Neuromodulation is defined as the influence on neural activity through targeted delivery of physical energy (electrical, magnetic, acoustic) or chemical agents, aiming to restore normal neural function or modify it therapeutically. rTMS is the most important representative of non-invasive neuromodulation in modern psychiatry. Learn in detail [how rTMS works in practice](/en/rtms/core/kostos-kai-diadikasia).

Neuromodulation represents a fundamentally different therapeutic approach from pharmacology: instead of modifying chemistry, it modifies circuit physiology. This offers advantages where pharmacology fails — especially in TRD, treatment resistance, and disorders with clear neural circuit deficits.

### Mechanism — Categories of Neuromodulation

**1. Non-invasive:**
- **rTMS / TMS:** repetitive magnetic stimulation, forming the basis for [rTMS for depression and other indications](/en/rtms) (approved for TRD/OCD/Substance use disorder).
- **tDCS (Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation):** subthreshold constant current; does not directly induce action potentials – modifies membrane potential.
- **tACS (Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation):** alternating current — synchronization of specific oscillations.
- **Focused Ultrasound (FUS):** acoustic waves — targeting deeper structures without surgery.

**2. Minimally invasive:**
- **ECT (Electroconvulsive Therapy):** high efficacy in TRD; anesthesia mandatory.
- **MST (Magnetic Seizure Therapy):** TMS-induced seizure with a more localized effect than ECT.

**3. Invasive:**
- **DBS (Deep Brain Stimulation):** implanted electrodes (sgACC, nucleus accumbens) — for refractory cases.
- **VNS (Vagus Nerve Stimulation):** electrical stimulation of the vagus nerve — FDA-approved for TRD (long-term).
- **SCS (Spinal Cord Stimulation):** for chronic pain.

### Clinical Significance
The main non-invasive neuromodulation methods are applied. Selection among them is based on diagnostic profile, previous treatments, symptom severity, and parameters such as [DLPFC targeting](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/dlpfc). See more [frequently asked questions about neuromodulation](/en/rtms/core/syxnes).
