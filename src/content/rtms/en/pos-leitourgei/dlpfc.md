The Dorsolateral Prefrontal Cortex (DLPFC) is the primary target site in [rTMS for depression](/en/rtms/endixeis/katathlipsi). It is located in the lateral prefrontal cortex (Brodmann areas 9 and 46) and serves as a control hub for executive functions, emotional regulation, and working memory processing. These changes are achieved through [neuroplasticity of the DLPFC](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/neuroplasticity).

### Why it matters
In major depression, the left DLPFC consistently shows reduced metabolic activity and reduced functional connectivity with the Anterior Cingulate Cortex (ACC). Correcting this dysfunction via rTMS is the central mechanism of antidepressant action.

### Mechanism
- **L-DLPFC (left):** Underactive in MDD → target of activating protocols (HF-rTMS, iTBS).
- **R-DLPFC (right):** Relatively overactive → target of inhibitory protocols (LF-rTMS, cTBS) or for anxiety and [rTMS for OCD](/en/rtms/endixeis/ocd).
- The DLPFC exercises top-down control over the ACC through the cortico-limbic pathway — when this connection is restored, negative emotion processing normalizes.
- **Neural basis:** Glutamatergic L5 pyramidal neurons and GABAergic circuits in the DLPFC.
- Stimulation of the L-DLPFC increases dopaminergic and serotonergic neurotransmission indirectly through cortico-striatal circuits.

### Clinical Significance
Targeting: Classically using the 5 cm rule (5 cm anterior to the motor hotspot), and currently with MRI-based neuronavigation for more accurate localization. Precise targeting of the functionally connected DLPFC (anti-correlated with sgACC) is associated with improved clinical outcomes. See the [detailed procedure in the clinic](/en/rtms/core/kostos-kai-diadikasia). Read more [frequently asked questions about targeting](/en/rtms/core/syxnes).
