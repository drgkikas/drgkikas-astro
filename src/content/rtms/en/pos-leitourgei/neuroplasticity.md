Neuroplasticity is the brain's ability to reorganize synapses, neural circuits, and functional connections in response to stimuli. Specifically, rTMS utilizes exactly this mechanism. Learn in detail [how rTMS induces plasticity](/en/rtms/core/ti-einai). rTMS induces long-term changes in synaptic strength that persist beyond the duration of the stimulation itself. Therefore, rTMS does not simply stimulate neurons temporarily — it brings about lasting changes in brain function.

### Why it matters
The application of [rTMS for depression](/en/rtms/endixeis/katathlipsi) targets exactly this: depression is associated with reduced neuroplasticity, especially in the prefrontal cortex and the hippocampus. Consequently, rTMS does not just "knock" on neurons — it restores the brain's ability to change. This process is based on the initial [motor threshold measurement](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/motor-threshold). As a result, clinical improvement often appears gradually, after the 2nd–3rd week, and remains after the end of treatment. Furthermore, this explains why a full cycle of sessions is necessary for maximum and lasting effect.

### Mechanism
- **LTP (Long-Term Potentiation):** High-frequency protocols (≥5 Hz, [iTBS](/en/rtms/core/ti-einai)) strengthen synaptic transmission — repeated stimulation increases AMPA receptor density and BDNF expression.
- **LTD (Long-Term Depression):** Low-frequency protocols (≤1 Hz) reduce excitability — useful in structures with pathological hyperexcitability (e.g., increased anxiety).
- **BDNF upregulation:** rTMS increases levels of Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor, which promotes neuron survival and the formation of new synapses.
- **Synaptogenesis:** New dendritic spines appear after repeated stimulation in animal models.
- **Network-level remodeling:** Effects propagate to connected structures through cortico-subcortical loops (DLPFC → ACC → amygdala → hippocampus).

### Clinical Significance
Patients with MDD show reduced activity through [DLPFC targeting](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/dlpfc). Specifically, rTMS reverses these findings through neuroplasticity mechanisms. Furthermore, fMRI studies document increased functional connectivity between DLPFC and sgACC after rTMS. Therefore, neuroplasticity is the central mechanism through which rTMS achieves lasting clinical improvement in the patient.
