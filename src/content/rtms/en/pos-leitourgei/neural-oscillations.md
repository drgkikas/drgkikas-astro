Neural oscillations are rhythmic fluctuations of electrical activity in neural networks. Specifically, each oscillation carries a specific frequency and amplitude. Every brain function — such as memory, sleep, and stimulus processing — is connected to different frequency bands. Therefore, rTMS utilizes these bands through the entrainment phenomenon. This is a targeted [neuromodulation of rhythms](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/neuromodulation) that changes the functional dynamics of the brain.

### Why it matters
The relationship between [iTBS and theta rhythm](/en/rtms/core/ti-einai) (4–8 Hz) represents the physiological rhythm of the hippocampal circuit during memory and emotional processing. Consequently, protocols that "speak the brain's language" utilize endogenous neuroplasticity mechanisms instead of overcoming them with a simple flood of pulses.

### Mechanism
- **Delta (0.5–4 Hz):** The delta rhythm dominates in deep sleep and recovery. Consequently, LF-rTMS (~1 Hz) induces delta-type inhibition.
- **Theta (4–8 Hz):** The theta rhythm supports hippocampal memory and emotional processing. It also forms the basis of iTBS.
- **Alpha (8–13 Hz):** The alpha rhythm promotes inhibition and calmness. As a result, clinicians use rTMS at an alpha frequency for [chronic pain](/en/rtms/endixeis/ponos).
- **Beta (13–30 Hz):** The beta rhythm controls motor function and cognitive processing. Simultaneously, patients with OCD and anxiety show excessive beta rhythm.
- **Gamma (30–100 Hz):** The gamma rhythm supports conscious processing and binding. Furthermore, researchers are investigating gamma coordination as a treatment for Alzheimer's disease.
- **Entrainment:** Repeated stimulation at a rhythm's frequency strengthens and "locks" that rhythm in the cortex.

### Clinical Significance
Depression is associated with increased theta in the ACC and decreased alpha in the left DLPFC. Consequently, iTBS targets precisely this dysfunction. Furthermore, researchers are developing EEG-synchronized rTMS (closed-loop), which applies stimulation at exactly the right phase of the endogenous rhythm. Thus, the system achieves maximum efficacy with minimal pulses. Read more [frequently asked questions about rTMS function](/en/rtms/core/syxnes).
