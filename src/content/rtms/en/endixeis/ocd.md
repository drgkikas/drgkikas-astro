Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) is characterized by repetitive, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and/or repetitive behaviors (compulsions) that cause intense distress and functional impairment. It affects 2–3% of the general population and is among the most treatment-resistant psychiatric disorders — only 40–60% of patients respond adequately to a combination of SSRIs and Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT/ERP). The FDA granted De Novo approval for rTMS in OCD in 2018. Learn in detail [how rTMS works](/en/rtms/core/ti-einai).

---

## Why it matters

Approximately 25–40% of patients with OCD remain resistant even after multiple medication trials and high-intensity psychotherapy. Therapeutic alternatives are limited: surgical [neuromodulation](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/neuromodulation) (DBS), high-dose clomipramine, or augmentation strategies. rTMS — especially Deep TMS (dTMS) — enters as a non-invasive intermediate option, clinically approved and with proven efficacy. Learn about [cost and procedure for OCD](/en/rtms/core/kostos-kai-diadikasia).

## Mechanism

- **Neurobiological basis of OCD**: Dysfunction of the cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical (CSTC) circuit — hyperactivity of the orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) and the caudate, resulting in a "stuck" loop of repetitive thought/behavior.
- **rTMS target in OCD**: Low-frequency (LF, 1 Hz) inhibition of the right [DLPFC](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/dlpfc) or the supplementary motor area (SMA) — to regulate compulsive motor drive. Here too, [personalization of treatment through motor threshold](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/motor-threshold) is essential. Alternatively: deep TMS targeting of the OFC/ACC.
- **Symptom provocation**: Activation of OCD circuits a few minutes before TMS application enhances efficacy — leveraging the activation state for targeted [neuromodulation and plasticity](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/neuroplasticity).

---

## Clinical Significance

The pivotal RCT that led to FDA approval (Carmi et al., 2019) showed a statistically significant reduction in Y-BOCS score (~38.1% reduction) in the active dTMS group with symptom provocation, compared with placebo. Meta-analyses across all rTMS/dTMS protocols in OCD show a moderate to strong effect (effect size ~0.6–0.8). Response is better in patients with a dominant "symmetry/ordering" dimension and poorer in those with "hoarding." See more [frequently asked questions about rTMS in OCD](/en/rtms/core/syxnes).
