Chronic pain is defined as pain that persists or recurs for more than 3 months, beyond the expected healing time. It includes heterogeneous conditions: neuropathic pain, fibromyalgia, and chronic migraine. rTMS — and specifically targeting the primary motor cortex (M1) — is recognized as a valid therapeutic option for resistant chronic pain syndromes. Learn in detail [how rTMS works](/en/rtms/core/ti-einai).

---

## Why it matters

Chronic pain is one of the most common causes of disability and social exclusion worldwide. Pharmacotherapy (opioids, anticonvulsants, tricyclic antidepressants) has significant limitations: addiction, tolerance, and side effects. Neuropathic pain in particular — due to central sensitization — often responds minimally to conventional analgesics. rTMS provides non-invasive analgesia by modifying central pain processing mechanisms. See in detail [why we choose rTMS for pain](/en/rtms/).

---

## Mechanism

- **Central sensitization**: In chronic pain, central neural circuits (thalamus, somatosensory cortex, anterior cingulate gyrus) are in a state of hyperexcitability — rTMS reverses this dysfunction, enhancing [neuroprotective action and neuroplasticity](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/neuroplasticity).
- **M1 as target**: High-frequency (HF, 5–20 Hz) stimulation of the primary motor cortex (M1) — following [motor threshold measurement](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/motor-threshold) — activates cascades from the periaqueductal gray (PAG) to descending analgesic pathways, increasing endogenous opioids.
- **[DLPFC](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/dlpfc) as secondary target**: Adjunctive for comorbid depressive/anxiety symptoms that often coexist with chronic pain.
- **Neuropathic pain**: Strongest indications for post-herpetic neuralgia, trigeminal neuralgia, central post-stroke pain, and fibromyalgia.

---

## Clinical Significance

Meta-analyses (Lefaucheur et al., 2014, 2020 — IFCN guidelines) classify HF-rTMS M1 as Level A evidence for neuropathic pain of central or peripheral origin. Studies in fibromyalgia show a 30–50% reduction in the VAS pain scale after a full treatment course. Results typically recur after weeks to months — leading to maintenance session protocols.
