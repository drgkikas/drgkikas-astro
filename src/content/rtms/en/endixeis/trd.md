Treatment-Resistant Depression (TRD) is defined as major depressive disorder that has not responded adequately to at least two courses of antidepressant treatment of adequate dosage and duration (≥4–6 weeks each) administered during the current depressive episode. It is the primary indication for [rTMS for depression](/en/rtms/endixeis/katathlipsi).

---

### Why it matters
Approximately 30% of patients with MDD do not respond satisfactorily to pharmacotherapy (STAR*D trial data). These patients bear a disproportionately large burden of disability, suicide risk, and healthcare costs. rTMS, especially accelerated [iTBS](/en/rtms/core/ti-einai) (SAINT protocol), offers rapid antidepressant action in a population with few alternatives. Learn about the [detailed procedure in the clinic](/en/rtms/core/kostos-kai-diadikasia).

---

### Mechanism
- **Neurobiological basis of TRD:** reduced activity in the prefrontal cortex, decreased brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), glutamatergic dysfunction (NMDA receptor hypofunction), and impaired [neuroplasticity](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/neuroplasticity).
- **Pharmacological factors:** resistance due to genetic variants of cytochrome P450 (CYP2D6, CYP2C19 poor/ultrarapid metabolizers), P-glycoprotein polymorphisms (transport across the blood-brain barrier – BBB), and pharmacodynamic tolerance.
- **rTMS bypasses pharmacological resistance mechanisms:** it acts directly on neural circuits aiming to [activate neuroplasticity](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/neuroplasticity).

---

### Clinical Significance
In patients with TRD, rTMS achieves response rates of ~50–55% and remission rates of ~30–35% (meta-analyses). The SAINT protocol (accelerated iTBS, 50 sessions/5 days) showed remission rates of 79% in an open-label study (Cole et al., 2020) — a result partially replicated in RCTs. Crucial to success is the [individualized measurement of motor threshold](/en/rtms/pos-leitourgei/motor-threshold). See more [frequently asked questions about effectiveness](/en/rtms/core/syxnes). Pharmacogenetic (PGx) assessment before rTMS helps optimize concurrent pharmacotherapy.
